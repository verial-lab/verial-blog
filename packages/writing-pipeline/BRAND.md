# Verial — Brand Reference

> **Source of truth:** The full brand document lives in the OpenClaw workspace PARA system at `PARA/Areas/verial-blog/brand.md`. This file is a summary and pointer — always defer to the source.

## Mission

**Truth-seeking. Applied.**

Distill lessons from the technology frontier into wisdom — valuable, high-density insights that compound. Never "the truth" — always *truth-seeking*, because that implies there are always more questions to ask.

## Three Pillars

1. **Systems & Complexity** — How complex systems work, fail, and scale. Software architecture as a lens for understanding organizations, economies, and nature.
2. **The New Literacy** — Technology as the new interface layer for human capability. AI as a layer on top of the application layer. What it means that everyone now interfaces with computation through words.
3. **Truth-Seeking & Philosophy** — Epistemology applied to building. How do we know what's true in complex systems? First-principles thinking and the discipline of seeking without claiming to have arrived.

## Voice

- **Tone model:** Dario Amodei — technical enough to be credible, accessible enough to be useful
- **Author lens:** Innovation Technologist & Strategic Advisor — a practitioner with altitude
- **Sharp, warm, dense** — personality without performance, directness without coldness

## Brand Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any Verial content, check:

- [ ] **Mission-aligned?** Does it seek truth and apply it?
- [ ] **Audience-appropriate?** Would a non-engineer still get value?
- [ ] **Voice-consistent?** Sharp, warm, dense? No filler or hedging?
- [ ] **Pillar-mapped?** Does it sit clearly in one of the three pillars?
- [ ] **Concrete?** Are there real examples grounding the ideas?
- [ ] **Worth bookmarking?** Would someone come back to this?
