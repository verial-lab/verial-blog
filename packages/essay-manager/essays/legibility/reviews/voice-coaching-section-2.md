# Voice Coaching — Section 2: The New Failure Mode

_Captured: 2026-02-27_

## Core Message (as spoken)
AI generates code at a speed and volume that outpaces human comprehension — the bottleneck has shifted from *writing* code to *understanding* it, and that gap is the new risk.

---

## Lines to Keep (Nearly Verbatim)

**Train metaphor setup — strong, stop early:**
> "driving a powerful locomotive train filled with lots of flammable cargo, driving at full speed in the dark, blind"

The instinct is right. Kill it at 2-3 sentences. Do not explain the train further.

**Clean consequence line — keep this:**
> "you have a buzzer that tells you when to stop at your next station, but if anything happens between stations, it's gonna be bad"

**The actual thesis, nearly said clearly:**
> "the one thing that's definitely not changing barely any or at all compared to AI — that is human bandwidth... the number of hours we have per day"

One more pass and this becomes a strong spoken line.

---

## Three Coaching Tips

1. **Kill the metaphor before it kills itself.** When you catch yourself saying "and like" or "you know what I mean" more than twice inside a metaphor — you've already made the point. Practice a hard exit: *"You're driving blind. That's the situation."* Move on. The audience's imagination does the rest.

2. **Lead with the bottleneck claim, then earn it.** Right now you build for several minutes before landing the actual insight. State the claim first: *"The bottleneck has moved. It's no longer about writing code, it's about understanding it."* Then bring in the train and the bandwidth observation. Lead with the conclusion, follow with the evidence.

3. **Sentence bankruptcy discipline.** When a sentence goes sideways, stop completely. Pause. Start fresh. A clean pause sounds authoritative. A rescued fragment sounds uncertain. You said "Scratch that" once — that was the right instinct. Do it more.
