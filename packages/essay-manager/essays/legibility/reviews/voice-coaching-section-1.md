# Voice Coaching — Section 1: Hook

_Captured: 2026-02-28_

## Core Message (as spoken)

When AI amplifies your output to the scale of 100 engineers, legibility stops being a best practice and becomes the central discipline that determines whether you stay in control of what you build.

## Lines to Keep (Nearly Verbatim)

**"I would start to feel bad, feel frustrated, kind of have some self-doubt."**
Keep this. It's honest and specific enough to be credible. It earns the turn. Don't dress it up.

**"Today I do feel confident. I do feel confident about how to build systems that we can better understand."**
The repetition here is actually good — it lands as conviction, not redundancy. Keep the double "I do feel confident." That rhythm works in spoken form.

**"If a single person is going to be doing the work of 100 software engineers from 2022, then we definitely have to take our ability to understand and feel confident about that system seriously."**
This is the thesis. It's strong. It just needs to arrive sooner and cleaner — it should be the knockout punch of the hook.

## Three Coaching Tips

1. **Cut to the turn faster.** You spend too long in the problem before pivoting. The listener needs to feel your frustration, but they don't need to fully understand it yet — that's what the body of the essay is for. In the hook, feel the pain for two sentences, then pivot. Right now the pivot takes nearly 150 words to arrive.

2. **Name legibility earlier and harder.** The word "legibility" doesn't land with force until the end of the second paragraph. For a hook, that's too late. Practice speaking the word within the first 30-40 words of the turn: *"That's when I started taking legibility seriously."* Drop it early, let the rest pay it off.

3. **Cut the meditation entirely when speaking.** "Some great meditation, just trying to sit with my feelings" introduces a register shift at the worst possible moment — right before your most important idea. In your spoken delivery, jump from "frustration" directly to "the right idea started to arrive." Save the full story for somewhere it won't undercut the argument.

## What Belongs Here vs. What to Move

**Keep:**
- The emotional low (frustration, self-doubt, stalled projects)
- The turn (something shifted, confidence returned)
- The thesis statement (the 100-engineers line)
- First mention of legibility as the answer

**Move to body of essay:**
- The explanation of what legibility actually *is* — that's Section 2+ material
- Comparison to traceability, observability, agentic systems — that's Section 2 or later
- "Legibility was important before 2022 but now it's front and center" — this is a thesis expansion, not a hook beat

**Cut entirely:**
- The meditation reference
- "Which isn't a bad thing, but..." — hedging undercuts authority; hook is not the place to qualify
- Any sentence containing "in a way" or "kind of" — these bleed confidence from delivery in real time
